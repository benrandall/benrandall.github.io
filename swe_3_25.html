CS373 Spring 2018: Benjamin Randall

3/25/2018

What did you do this past week?

This past week my team and I worked on finishing up the second phase of our IDB project. We had a lot more problems figuring out testing frameworks than we had planned for, and deployment was very difficult. Luckily, I had a great team who pulled more than their weight. Outside of this class, I dedicated a lot of time to a DJ set I have been preparing, which went really well.

What's in your way?

I feel like I got over some major roadblocks for now. The past week was busy for sure, so anything on the horizon feels relatively small. I have a long essay I have to prepare for in a government class. It has been a long time since I've written anything close to eight pages, so the thought of that process is pretty intimidating. Other than a few small assignments, there's not much in my way!

What will you do next week?

I have a lot to catch up on next week in this class and out. I need to catch up on readings for this class and look into the specifics of SQL and relational databases. Sounds riveting. I also have to start looking at IDB phase three. In other classes I have tests, quizzes, and assignments I need to prepare for.

What's my experience of the class?

This phase of the IDB project was definitely hard, but I'm not sure if it was harder than the first phase. Again, I have a really great team who puts their hard work and experience to good use. It is great to be able to learn from them as the project goes. There is a definite rhythym to the class that I have picked up on, which makes a lot of things easier. If everything continues to my expectations, I think I could see myself solidly landing an A in this class.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

I am sure that many people heard about Dropbox's stock jumping 36 percent on its NASDAQ debut. This is significantly the biggest tech IPO since Snapchat. Dropbox ended the day of trading, under the ticker symbol “DBX,” with a market valuation of around $10 billion. I was reading about how investors are all over the "subscription" model of a product, like Netflix or a paid Dropbox account. It provides a solid, predictable stream of revenue that makes companies like Dropbox a safe investment. Dropbox started 10 years ago as a small startup in the San Francisco-based Y Combinator incubator program; it makes me wonder if I'll see any of my fellow students in similar news someday soon!
